## Part II: The Abraham Narrative

- **The Call of Abram** (Gen 12:1--9) <br> Call to leave Haran; promise of land, nation, and blessing.
- **Abram in Egypt** (Gen 12:10--20) <br> Famine flight; Abram lies about Sarai to Pharaoh to save himself.
- **Abram and Lot Separate** (Gen 13) <br> Lot chooses the fertile plain near Sodom; Abram stays in Canaan.
- **The War of Kings & Melchizedek** (Gen 14) <br> Abram rescues Lot; blessed by Melchizedek (King of Salem).
- **The Covenant of the Torch** (Gen 15) <br> God formalizes the promise; prophesies 400 years of slavery.
- **Hagar and Ishmael** (Gen 16) <br> Sarai uses Hagar as a surrogate; Ishmael born; Hagar flees and returns.
- **The Covenant of Circumcision** (Gen 17) <br> Names changed (Abraham/Sarah); circumcision commanded as a sign.
- **The Three Visitors** (Gen 18:1--15) <br> Prophecy that Sarah will have a son within a year.
- **Abraham Intercedes for Sodom** (Gen 18:16--33) <br> Abraham bargains with God to spare the righteous.
- **Sodom and Gomorrah** (Gen 19) <br> Destruction of cities; Lot's escape; incestuous origin of Moab/Ammon.
- **Abraham and Abimelech** (Gen 20) <br> Abraham lies about Sarah to the King of Gerar.
- **The Birth of Isaac** (Gen 21:1--7) <br> Fulfillment of the promise; Isaac born to aged Sarah.
- **Expulsion of Hagar and Ishmael** (Gen 21:8--21) <br> Conflict leads to their exile; God preserves them in the desert.
- **Treaty at Beersheba** (Gen 21:22--34) <br> Abraham secures water rights and peace with Abimelech.
- **The Binding of Isaac (Akedah)** (Gen 22) <br> God tests Abraham's faith on Mt. Moriah; the ram substitute.
- **The Death of Sarah** (Gen 23) <br> Purchase of the Cave of Machpelah for burial.
- **A Wife for Isaac** (Gen 24) <br> Servant sent to Mesopotamia to find Rebekah.
- **Death of Abraham** (Gen 25:1--11) <br> Abraham marries Keturah, dies, and is buried.
- * **[List] The Descendants of Ishmael** (Gen 25:12--18) <br> _Summary:_ List of the 12 tribal rulers of Ishmael. <br> _Future Context:_ Ancestors of Arab tribes; future conflict with Israel.
