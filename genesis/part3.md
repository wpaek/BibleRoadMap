## Part III: The Isaac and Jacob Narrative

- **Jacob and Esau Born** (Gen 25:19--26) <br> Twins born; prophecy that "the older will serve the younger."
- **Esau Sells His Birthright** (Gen 25:27--34) <br> Esau trades spiritual inheritance for stew.
- **Isaac in Gerar** (Gen 26) <br> Isaac lies about Rebekah; dispute over wells; covenant renewal.
- **The Stolen Blessing** (Gen 27) <br> Jacob deceives blind Isaac to take the firstborn blessing.
- **Jacob's Ladder** (Gen 28) <br> Flight to Haran; dream of stairway to heaven at Bethel.
- **Jacob Marries Leah and Rachel** (Gen 29:1--30) <br> Labor for Laban; deception in marriage.
- **The Battle of Births** (Gen 29:31--30:24) <br> Wives/servants compete; birth of 11 tribes.
- **Jacob's Flocks Increase** (Gen 30:25--43) <br> Jacob uses breeding techniques to gain wealth from Laban.
- **Jacob Flees Laban** (Gen 31) <br> Secret departure; Laban pursues; Treaty of Mizpah.
- **Jacob Wrestles with God** (Gen 32) <br> Wrestling at Peniel; name changed to **Israel**.
- **Reunion with Esau** (Gen 33) <br> Peaceful reconciliation between brothers.
- **The Defiling of Dinah** (Gen 34) <br> Rape of Dinah; massacre of Shechem by Simeon and Levi.
- **Return to Bethel** (Gen 35:1--15) <br> Altar built; foreign gods removed.
- **Deaths and Birth** (Gen 35:16--29) <br> Rachel dies (birth of Benjamin); Isaac dies.
- **Reuben's Sin** (Gen 35:22) <br> Reuben sleeps with Bilhah (loses firstborn status).
- * **[List] The Genealogy of Esau (Edom)** (Gen 36) <br> _Summary:_ Kings and chiefs of Edom. <br> _Future Context:_ Perpetual rivals to Israel (e.g., Herod the Great).
